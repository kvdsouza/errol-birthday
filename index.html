New Orleans → Memphis → Nashville → Louisville

11 days. 4 cities. 1 family. Infinite memories.

1
Wednesday, April 30
Arrive New Orleans
Touch down in the Crescent City — let the music find you
🎷
+

You're landing late, but New Orleans doesn't sleep. Check into your hotel, drop your bags, and walk straight to Frenchmen Street. The bands will still be playing. This is your first taste of what Errol's week is made of.

🎷 Errol
Frenchmen Street Live Jazz
The real jazz scene. Multiple clubs side by side — Snug Harbor, The Spotted Cat, d.b.a. Live music till 4am. This is your city tonight.
👨‍👩‍👦‍👦 Everyone
Evening Beignets at Café Du Monde
Open 24 hours. Powdered sugar cloud beignets and café au lait. A New Orleans rite of passage — do it the first night.
🍽️ Late Night Eats
Café Du Monde (beignets) Clover Grill (24hr diner) Verti Marte (deli)
2–4
Thursday May 1 – Saturday May 3
New Orleans Jazz Fest
The world's greatest music festival — and it's Errol's birthday week
🎺
+

Jazz Fest at the New Orleans Fair Grounds is a bucket-list experience — 12 stages, incredible food, art vendors, and the city's soul on full display. Three full days here. Take your time, split up and reconvene, soak it all in.

🎷 Errol
Jazz Fest Main Stages
Check the lineup when you arrive — Jazz Fest regularly features artists in the Herbie Hancock universe. The Jazz Tent and Blues Tent are must-stays. This is the centrepiece of the whole trip.
🎷 Errol
Preservation Hall
The most iconic jazz venue in America. Small, intimate, powerful. Buy tickets in advance — 3 shows a night. An easy evening ritual between festival days.
🏈 Jonathan
Caesar's Superdome Tour
Home of the Saints. One of the most storied venues in NFL history. Tour the stadium and walk the field. Worth the 2 hours.
🏈 Jonathan
Zephyr Field / Sports Scene
Check local sports schedules — minor league baseball and other events often run during Jazz Fest weekend. New Orleans has a passionate sports culture.
🎨 Maria
New Orleans Museum of Art (NOMA)
World-class collection including African, French, and American art. Beautiful sculpture garden. Half-day visit — take a quiet morning here before the festival heat.
🎨 Maria
Ogden Museum of Southern Art
Only museum dedicated to Southern art — vibrant, colourful, emotional. Jazz Fest nights feature live music in the galleries. Maria's favourite stop.
🗺️ Kyle
St. Louis Cemetery #1 Tour
New Orleans buries its dead above ground. Oldest cemetery in the city, steeped in Voodoo history and legend. Guided tours only — book ahead. Eerie and unforgettable.
🗺️ Kyle
Airboat Swamp Tour
An hour outside NOLA: alligators, cypress trees, Spanish moss. A completely different Louisiana ecosystem. Book a dawn or dusk tour for the best wildlife sightings.
👨‍👩‍👦‍👦 Everyone
Garden District Walk
Stroll the grand antebellum mansions on St. Charles Avenue. Ride the historic streetcar. Stop at Commander's Palace for the famous jazz brunch — one of America's great restaurant experiences.
👨‍👩‍👦‍👦 Everyone
French Quarter & Bourbon Street
Walk Jackson Square, browse the art vendors, eat a muffuletta at Central Grocery. Save Bourbon Street for one big night — it earns its reputation.
🍽️ Must Eat in New Orleans
Commander's Palace (jazz brunch) Dooky Chase's (legendary NOLA) Galatoire's (French Quarter institution) August (fine dining) Cochon (Cajun) Domilise's Po-Boys Central Grocery muffuletta Hansen's Sno-Bliz (shaved ice)
🌙 Every night: Frenchmen Street for live jazz. Different bands each night. This is where the locals go after Jazz Fest winds down. Plan for at least 2–3 late nights here.
5
Sunday, May 4
Last Day in New Orleans
Slow morning, last tastes, final jazz — pack tonight
🌅
+

One more day in the city before the road trip begins. Sleep in, walk the Bywater neighborhood, and have a proper farewell dinner. Jazz Fest weekend finale.

👨‍👩‍👦‍👦 Everyone
Bywater Neighborhood Stroll
Hip, artistic, colourful. Street murals everywhere, locally-owned coffee shops, the city's creative community. Great morning walk.
🎷 Errol
Final Jazz Fest Day
Jazz Fest Sunday — often features the biggest headliners. Make it count. The closing sets are usually legendary.
👨‍👩‍👦‍👦 Everyone
Farewell Dinner: Dooky Chase's
Louis Armstrong ate here before every Jazz Fest performance. Obama visited. Civil rights history, incredible Creole food, and a New Orleans institution since 1941. Book ahead.
6
Monday, May 5
Baton Rouge
Death Valley, LSU, and a bayou sunset
🐊
+

Drive 80 miles west up the Mississippi River. Baton Rouge was the site of a crucial Civil War battle and is home to one of the most passionate college football cultures in America. Arrive by afternoon, explore, stay the night.

🏈 Jonathan
Tiger Stadium (Death Valley)
LSU's home field — one of the loudest stadiums in the world. No game today, but walk around the outside. During a game it holds 102,000. Jonathan will feel it just standing there.
🗺️ Kyle
Old Louisiana State Capitol
Mark Twain called it a "monstrosity." It's actually stunning — Gothic Revival, stained glass rotunda, wild political history. Free and unique.
🗺️ Kyle
USS Kidd Veterans Museum
A perfectly preserved WWII destroyer docked on the Mississippi River. You can walk the entire ship. Remarkable piece of floating history.
🎨 Maria
LSU Rural Life Museum
25-acre outdoor folk art and architecture museum — original plantation buildings, Acadian structures, folk art collection. Maria's afternoon highlight.
🍽️ Dinner in Baton Rouge
Parrain's Seafood (local institution) Drusilla Seafood (casual, excellent) Strops (boudin & crawfish)
7
Tuesday, May 6
Natchez Trace → Port Gibson → Memphis
One of America's great scenic drives — history at every mile
🌊
+

This is Kyle's dream day. Drive north on the Natchez Trace Parkway — a stunning 444-mile national parkway following the ancient trail that linked New Orleans to Nashville. No commercial traffic, no billboards. Stop at Port Gibson, one of the best-preserved antebellum towns in the South. Push on to Memphis by evening.

🗺️ Kyle
Natchez Trace Parkway
A Native American path turned Civil War route turned national treasure. Forested, serene, dotted with historical markers. The drive IS the destination.
👨‍👩‍👦‍👦 Everyone
Port Gibson — "Too Beautiful to Burn"
Ulysses Grant refused to burn it during the Civil War. Walk the main street lined with antebellum churches — including the famous church with a golden hand pointing toward heaven instead of a steeple.
🗺️ Kyle
Vicksburg National Military Park
One of the most significant Civil War battlefields in America. The siege of Vicksburg turned the tide of the war. Drive the 16-mile battlefield loop — haunting and incredible.
🎨 Maria
Natchez — Antebellum Mansion Trail
If you have time: Natchez has more antebellum mansions than anywhere in America. Stanton Hall, Melrose, Longwood (the unfinished octagonal wonder). An hour detour worth making.
🚗 Drive time: Baton Rouge to Memphis via Natchez Trace is about 5–6 hours with stops. Leave by 9am, arrive Memphis early evening. Dinner on Beale Street tonight.
8
Wednesday, May 7
Memphis
Elvis, civil rights, the blues, and the best ribs on earth
🎸
+

Memphis punches well above its weight. This is where rock and roll was born, where the civil rights movement made its most painful sacrifice, and where BBQ becomes religion. Full day here — split up by interest in the morning, reconvene for a Beale Street evening.

🎷 Errol
Sun Studio
Where Elvis, Johnny Cash, Jerry Lee Lewis, and B.B. King recorded their first songs. Still an active recording studio. The tour is electric — you stand on the same floor where rock and roll was invented.
🎷 Errol
Beale Street Live Music
B.B. King's Blues Club, Rum Boogie, Blues City Cafe — outdoor stages, indoor clubs, music spilling onto the street. Errol's second great music night of the trip.
🏈 Jonathan
FedExForum
Home of the Memphis Grizzlies. Check if there's a playoff game — NBA playoffs run through May. Even if not, do the arena tour. Grit Grind culture is palpable.
👨‍👩‍👦‍👦 Everyone
National Civil Rights Museum
Built around the Lorraine Motel where Dr. King was assassinated. The most powerful museum in America. Allow 3–4 hours. Deeply emotional. Non-negotiable for this trip.
🎨 Maria
Memphis Brooks Museum of Art
Tennessee's oldest and largest art museum. Strong collection of Italian Renaissance, French Impressionist, and American art. Beautiful building in Overton Park.
👨‍👩‍👦‍👦 Everyone
Graceland
Elvis's home. Bizarre, kitschy, and legitimately fascinating. Even non-fans leave moved. The jungle room, the gold records, the grave in the garden. American mythology made physical.
🗺️ Kyle
Mississippi River Overlook
You've been following this river for days. Stand at Tom Lee Park or Mud Island and really take it in. The Memphis bridge at sunset is one of the great American views.
🍽️ Memphis BBQ (a serious matter)
Rendezvous (dry ribs, institution since 1948) Central BBQ (best overall) Gus's Fried Chicken (legendary) Cozy Corner (BBQ Cornish hen — try it) The Arcade Restaurant (Elvis's diner)
9–10
Thursday–Friday, May 8–9
Nashville
Music City — honky tonks, history, and hot chicken
🤠
+

Three-hour drive from Memphis. Nashville has reinvented itself as one of America's most exciting cities — deep music roots, explosive food scene, genuine culture. Two nights here means you can actually breathe and explore properly.

🎷 Errol
Country Music Hall of Fame
Even for jazz fans, this museum is extraordinary — the story of American music told through artifacts, recordings, and immersive exhibitions. Half a day easily. The connection between jazz, blues, and country is the throughline.
🎷 Errol
Ryman Auditorium
The "Mother Church of Country Music." The acoustics are legendary. Try to catch a show — they book everything from country to jazz to classical. If nothing's on, do the daytime tour.
🏈 Jonathan
Nashville Predators (NHL Playoffs)
If the Preds made the playoffs, Bridgestone Arena in May is electric. Check the schedule — a playoff game in Nashville is a bucket-list sports experience. Even a regular game is great.
🏈 Jonathan
Nashville SC / Nissan Stadium
MLS soccer runs through May. Nashville SC matches are huge community events. Nissan Stadium (Titans' home) tour is also solid for a pro football fan.
🎨 Maria
Cheekwood Botanical Garden
55 acres of stunning gardens on a historic estate. May is peak bloom season — tulips, iris, early summer flowers. The art galleries on site are excellent too. Maria's favourite day.
🎨 Maria
Frist Art Museum
World-class rotating exhibitions in a stunning Art Deco building (former post office). Always something compelling on. Nashville's cultural jewel.
🗺️ Kyle
The Parthenon at Centennial Park
A full-scale replica of the Athenian Parthenon — built for Tennessee's 1897 centennial and now a permanent fixture. Inside: a 42-foot statue of Athena. Utterly bizarre and wonderful.
👨‍👩‍👦‍👦 Everyone
Lower Broadway Honky Tonks
Live music on every floor of every bar, noon to 2am, no cover charge. It's genuinely wild. Robert's Western World, Tootsies, Layla's. Pick a night and just wander.
🍽️ Nashville Must-Eats
Prince's Hot Chicken (original — prepare yourself) Hattie B's (hot chicken, shorter line) The Catbird Seat (tasting menu, book ahead) Josephine (farm-to-table, excellent) Biscuit Love (don't skip brunch) Arnold's Country Kitchen (meat + 3)
11
Saturday, May 10
Louisville
The Ali Center, Churchill Downs & Kentucky bourbon — the perfect finale
🥃
+

Two hours north of Nashville, Louisville is an underrated gem. The Kentucky Derby was May 2nd — you just missed it — but Churchill Downs is still open and the bourbon trail is in full swing. A beautiful final chapter.

👨‍👩‍👦‍👦 Everyone
Muhammad Ali Center
Six floors dedicated to Ali's life, legacy, and Louisville roots. Not just a sports museum — a civil rights and humanitarian monument. Errol will love this. One of the best museums in America, full stop.
🏈 Jonathan
Churchill Downs Tour
The Kentucky Derby may be over, but Churchill Downs runs races most Saturdays in May. Walk the famous track, stand in the Winner's Circle, watch live racing if available. Jonathan's dream afternoon.
👨‍👩‍👦‍👦 Everyone
Kentucky Bourbon Trail
Woodford Reserve or Buffalo Trace distillery — both within an hour. World-class tours, barrel tastings, and the story of American whiskey. Perfect for a birthday toast at the end of the trip.
🎨 Maria
Speed Art Museum
Kentucky's oldest and largest art museum, recently renovated. Strong permanent collection with European masters, American art, and Native American works. Beautiful building.
🗺️ Kyle
Louisville Slugger Museum
The legendary baseball bat manufacturer, still making bats in downtown Louisville. Watch them being made, learn the history, hold a bat that belonged to a Hall of Famer. Genuinely fascinating.
👨‍👩‍👦‍👦 Everyone
Final Birthday Dinner: Proof on Main
Inside the 21c Museum Hotel — contemporary art on every wall, outstanding Kentucky cuisine. The perfect final dinner for Errol's 60th. Art for Maria, great food for everyone, a memorable setting to mark the end of an unforgettable trip.
🍽️ Louisville Eats
Proof on Main (birthday dinner) Jack Fry's (Louisville institution) Garage Bar (wood-fired pizza + bourbon) Mayan Cafe (innovative, local) Gralehaus (coffee & brunch)
🥂 Trip finale toast: End the night with Kentucky bourbon poured neat. Raise a glass to Errol — 60 years, one incredible road trip, a family that loves him fiercely.

Here's to 60, Dad. 🥂

New Orleans jazz, Mississippi history, Memphis blues, Nashville honky tonks, and Louisville bourbon. That's a birthday worth having.
